About my research

Since 2012, I have been involved in various academic research projects, many of which I performed the leading role in theory crafting, data collection, data analysis, paper writing, and presenting. In my research, I use statistical techniques frequently utilized in the social sciences but less common in data science, such as structural equation modeling, multilevel modeling, allometric modeling, and social network analysis on survey and randomized experiment data sets.

Broadly, my research focuses on delivering actionable insights into organizational problems such as the following:

  • How to attract and manage diverse talent
  • How to manage turbulent organizational change
  • How to lead technology-reliant teams
  • How to accurately measure outlier performance
  • How to acquire and utilize advantageous network positions
